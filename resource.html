WHAT IS IMPLICIT BIAS?

WHY DOES THIS MATTER?

  1. Intersectionality: Coined by legal scholar Kimberlé Crenshaw, intersectionality refers to the way various forms of social identity (e.g., race, gender, class, religion, sexual orientation) intersect to create unique experiences of discrimination or privilege.
    • Complex Identities: People’s identities are complex, and each identity interacts with others to shape their experiences of privilege or oppression.
    • Inclusive Solutions: Intersectionality helps us design solutions that take into account different layers of oppression.
    • Understanding Oppression: It reveals how systems like racism, sexism, and classism work together to marginalize people.
  2. Implicit Bias: Refers to the unconscious attitudes or stereotypes that affect how we understand and treat others based on characteristics like race, gender, or socioeconomic status.
    • Unintentional Discrimination: Implicit biases can lead to unintended discrimination in areas like hiring, education, and law enforcement.
    • Impact on Decision-Making: These biases affect our judgments, which can have significant consequences, especially in high-stakes environments.
    • Inclusive Environments: Recognizing and addressing implicit bias helps create more inclusive and fair environments in workplaces, schools, and communities.
